    Early Age Of Riaz Ahmed Gohar Shahi

    His Holiness Gohar Shahi exhibited extraordinary humility and kindness from a very young age. According to his village friends, he was deeply considerate of all living beings, no matter how small. For instance, when walking along the edge of a water canal, while others might inadvertently step on ants or insects, His Holiness would take great care to avoid harming them. This compassion extended to humans as well. If a car or bus broke down on a road near his village, he would not simply ignore the stranded passengers. Instead, he would personally prepare food and tea and take it to the spot to assist them. Even as a young boy, His Holiness showed a profound commitment to helping the poor and those in need, demonstrating a genuine desire to ease the struggles of others. His actions reflected a heart full of empathy and a spirit devoted to service.
    From an early age, His Holiness Gohar Shahi displayed a profound interest in spirituality and Sufism. He would frequently visit the tombs of spiritual saints, seeking solace and divine connection in these sacred spaces as he pursued his quest for God. In his book, Roohani Safar , His Holiness recounts that as he reached the age of puberty, his yearning to become a saint reached its pinnacle. However, he found no clear path to fulfill this deep desire. Driven by an unwavering devotion, he would spend days and nights near the shrine of Baba Gohar Ali Shah , immersed in the recitation of religious verses, hoping to uncover the spiritual truths he sought so fervently. His dedication during these formative years laid the foundation for his profound spiritual journey.


    Spritual Journey of Riaz Ahmed Gohar Shahi

    At the age of 20, His Holiness Riaz Ahmed Gohar Shahi embarked on his spiritual journey to find God. His first destination was Lal Bagh, a renowned Sufi site in Pakistan, where he began his search. However, his efforts were short-lived due to illusions and distractions caused by Satan. This initial failure weakened his belief in divinity and sainthood, leaving him disillusioned with religious scholars and Sufis. Disheartened, His Holiness shifted his focus to worldly life. He married, settled down, and had three children. For about 12 years, he lived a conventional life until he began experiencing profound spiritual dreams. In these dreams, a mysterious figure appeared, urging him to renounce worldly attachments and dedicate himself to religious practices. After about a year of these dreams, the figure gave a final command: to leave worldly life within three days to purify his lower self. In 1975, following this divine instruction, His Holiness journeyed to Sehwan Sharif, the shrine of Sufi Saint Lal Shahbaz Qalandar. His Holiness recounted his first night at Sehwan Sharif. While engaging in zikr (chanting the name of God), he fell asleep and saw a gathering of saints in his dream. Among them was a tall, robust man with a striking appearance, including a large build and long mustaches. The saint pointed towards him and recited a Punjabi verse: "Asaan haan Qalandar dewaany lajpaal dy" ("We are the ecstatic followers of Qalandar, the protector of the helpless.") From that night onward, His Holiness's heartbeat began to rhythmically chant the name of God. Overwhelmed with joy, he realized that the God he had been searching for in jungles, caves, and distant places had been within his own heart all along.

    Incident in Jungle With Riaz Ahmed Gohar Shahi

    His Holiness shared a remarkable incident from the Laal Bagh jungle. He recounted that one day, a tall, elderly man appeared and silently stared at him before walking towards a nearby canal. Around 3 AM, the same man returned. In the darkness, his eyes glowed like fire, casting an eerie presence. As he approached closer, His Holiness felt an intense shock course through his body. At that very moment, His Holiness's heartbeat began chanting the name of God at a rapid pace. Suddenly, a radiant white ball of light emerged from his chest and shot towards the old man. The glowing orb struck the man, causing him to step back in pain. Angered, the man began hurling stones at His Holiness. In this extraordinary moment, His Holiness saw a figure, identical to himself, emerge from his own body. This spiritual form stood before the tall man while His Holiness's physical body became invisible to him. It seemed to be his spiritual body‚Äîa divine manifestation. The spiritual body began reciting something and blew its breath towards the tall man, inflicting pain on him. Eventually, flames started emerging from the tall man's mouth. In response, the spiritual body flew like a bird and perched atop a nearby palm tree. Enraged, the tall man threw stones, but his fiery breath and projectiles failed to reach the spiritual body. However, just as he approached, the spiritual body soared into the sky like an eagle, leaving the tall man staring in disbelief and defeat. Realizing his inability to confront this divine entity, the tall man left the jungle, bewildered and anxious.

This marked the emergence of nine other human-like figures resembling His Holiness.     Leaving Jungle

    After spending approximately three years in the jungle, His Holiness received a divine command from God to leave the jungle and return to Hyderabad. The instruction was clear: serve humanity by sharing the knowledge of God and helping people reconnect with the Divine. Upon returning to Hyderabad, His Holiness chose a serene spot near Jamshoro, behind the Textbook Board. There, he built a simple hut that became a sanctuary for those seeking solace and spiritual guidance. His Holiness dedicated himself to healing the emotionally distressed and purifying people's hearts through the practice of zikr (chanting the name of God). People who had previously encountered His Holiness during his time in Sehwan began visiting this new location to continue seeking his blessings and wisdom. Word of his presence spread quickly, and his humble hut started attracting students from universities and medical colleges. These young minds showed a profound interest in the spiritual teachings and practices of zikr that His Holiness imparted. However, this growing attention did not sit well with everyone. The principal of one of the universities, noticing the students' increasing visits, tried to stop them but failed to deter their determination. Frustrated, the principal eventually instructed his staff to dismantle the hut. Following these orders, the hut was torn down. Despite this setback, His Holiness remained unwavering in his mission. He relocated to a new place within Hyderabad and continued his sacred work. Undeterred by obstacles, he persisted in awakening people's hearts, guiding them towards a deeper connection with God, and spreading the message of love and divinity. His efforts inspired countless individuals, leaving an indelible mark on their spiritual journeys.
